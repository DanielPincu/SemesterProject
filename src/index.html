Current Exhibitions

NANNA DITZEL

TAKING DESIGN TO NEW HEIGHTS

Nanna Ditzel is one of the greatest Danish designers of the twentieth century. For the first time ever, her world of ideas and inspirations will be fully unfolded as Trapholt presents her total oeuvre in a densely sensuous array of furniture design, textiles, jewellery and immersive installations.


THE SCULPTURE
PARK

Architecture, art and nature come together in Trapholt’s sculpture park. Set in a scenic location overlooking the Kolding Fjord, Trapholt’s sculpture park offers a stunning extension of your museum visit. The sculpture park presents a collection of monumental works that relate directly to the surrounding architecture and nature.

RICHARD MORTENSEN

ROOM

By this time, the abstract artist Richard Mortensen (1910–93) had long been keenly interested in the interplay between painting and architecture. Mortensen proposed that if the architects behind the museum, Boye Lundgaard and Bente Aude, would design a room dedicated to Richard Mortensen’s art.

IN CLAY
AND PAPER

If you stroll down through the museum’s main walk, you will find yourself surrounded by large, strikingly thought-provoking works on paper and expressive ceramic sculptures by two internationally acclaimed artists: Danish Michael Kvium (b. 1955) and Norwegian Kjell Erik Killi-Olsen (b. 1952).

ARNE JACOBSEN

SUMMERHOUSE

But only few know that the Danish architect and designer also created a modular summerhouse. Known as Kubeflex, the design revolves around cube-shaped modules that can be combined to suit individual needs, enabling the house to change its layout and size as required.

Christmas at Trapholt

1/6

Embrace the spirit of the season at Trapholt Museum's enchanting Christmas exhibition, where the halls come alive with the magic of holiday traditions. Wander through artfully adorned galleries, each showcasing a diverse collection of Christmas-themed masterpieces spanning eras and styles. From classic nativity scenes to contemporary interpretations, the museum invites visitors to immerse themselves in a festive tapestry of colors, emotions, and creativity. This year, as the museum marks its Christmas milestone, patrons are invited to share in the joy, artistry, and community spirit that define this magical holiday celebration at Trapholt."
